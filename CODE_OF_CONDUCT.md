# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

MethodAtlas is a collaborative project developed by contributors from Egothor,
Accenture, and the wider open-source community.
We are committed to making participation a respectful, harassment-free
experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible
disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression,
level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal
appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We act in accordance with the
[Accenture Code of Business Ethics](https://www.accenture.com/us-en/about/ethics-compliance/code-business-ethics),
which calls for honesty, respect, and taking responsibility for our actions.

## Our standards

Behaviour that contributes to a positive environment includes:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully
- Acknowledging mistakes and learning from them
- Focusing on what is best for the community and for the people who rely on
  this tool in regulated environments
- Treating security disclosures with confidentiality and professionalism

Unacceptable behaviour includes:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Sexualised language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Publishing private information of any person (doxxing) without explicit consent
- Sharing confidential customer data, internal Accenture materials, or
  non-public security vulnerability details in public channels
- Any conduct that would violate applicable laws or professional obligations
  (including obligations arising from an employment or consulting agreement)

## Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable
behaviour and taking fair, corrective action in response to any behaviour they
deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code,
issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct,
and to temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviours they
consider harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull
requests, discussions, code reviews, and any other space where a person
represents the project or its community.
It also applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces
such as conferences, meetups, or social media.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be
reported to the project maintainers at **conduct@egothor.org**.
For Accenture employees, conduct concerns may additionally be raised through
the standard Accenture Ethics Hotline.

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.
The maintainers will review and investigate every report and respond in a way
that is appropriate to the circumstances.
Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may
face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other members of the
project leadership.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences:

| Severity | Description | Consequence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1 — Correction** | Minor inappropriate language or unprofessional behaviour | Private written warning; public apology may be requested |
| **2 — Warning** | Repeated or more serious violation | Temporary restriction from interaction with the project |
| **3 — Temporary ban** | Sustained, serious, or deliberate violation | Temporary ban from all project spaces |
| **4 — Permanent ban** | Pattern of violations, harassment, or aggression | Permanent exclusion from all project spaces |
